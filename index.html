Matt Petric is an urban policy nerd and mapping enthusiast living in Brooklyn, NY. During his day job, Matt works in data for NYC government. At night, Matt fiddles around with web maps and attends courses through Pratt Institute's Certificate in GIS and Design program.

NYC Street Parker

Street Parker helps New Yorkers find the best once-a-week alternate side parking spots. Streets are color coded by day of the week and users can employ filters to show only spots for particular weekdays.

This interactive map was featured on the Gothamist and has received over 1,000 Facebook likes.

Brooklyn, one (thousand) building(s) at a time

Since 2010, Suzanne Spellen has documented the stories behind the facades of Brooklyn's most (and sometimes least) beautiful buildings in near daily contributions to the Brownstoner blog. I created this web map to help make Spellen's entire catalog more accessible to curious borough dwellers who want to know more about the buildings they walk past each day.
